<p><p>The supermarket anti-theft device market encompasses three main types: Radio Frequency (RF), Acousto-Magnetic (AM), and Electromagnetic Wave (EM) systems. RF systems use electromagnetic fields to detect tags on merchandise, while AM systems utilize a combination of acoustic and magnetic technology for enhanced detection accuracy. EM systems rely on electromagnetic signals to activate and disable tags. Each technology offers distinct advantages in terms of detection range, cost, and effectiveness, catering to varying retail security needs and preferences.</p></p>
